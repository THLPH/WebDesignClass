Biographical Summary

I am a Computer Science Bachelor's student operating at the intersection of high-level software and physical hardware. My background is rooted in the belief that engineering isn't just about writing code; it's about validating how that code interacts with the real world.

By combining embedded systems logic with hands-on electromechanical rework, I prioritize the reliability of physical interfaces. Whether it's diagnosing voltage backfeeding in automotive electronics or deploying headless Linux appliances, my work ensures systems are functional, predictable, and resilient.

Outside of technical development, I explore urban planning, 3D printing, and automotive modification. This perspective informs my work, leading to solutions that respect physical constraints and user needs.
